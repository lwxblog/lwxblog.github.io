Covid-19 and Social Responsibility or Lack Thereof

lwx 13 Mar 2020 on General

As Covid-19 spreads, the lack of social responsibility in Singapore is accentuated. While it has always bothered me, even before this highly infectious virus. The dangers of irresponsible behaviour has never been more apparent and detestable.

I am not saying that all Singaporeans lack civic mindedness, it is an indisputable fact that amongst us is a minority that pays no heed to personal hygenie and I suspect some takes pride in being unhygenic, mocking at the ones whom actually takes the situation seriously.

What prompted me to write about this was when I walked past my neighbour, who has been coughing quite badly for more than a week now, and could be under quarantine yet insists on smoking at the common area or going out with his friends, he deliberately coughed in my direction a couple of times. I strongly feel that he is trying to spread his virus to me.

Let’s take a step back

Living in a HDB, I am no stranger to second-hand smoke coming from my neighbour who loves to smoke at the common area, his family likes to place their stuff at the shared space between our units. The sense of entitlement and their slow but definite encroaching was a telltale sign. They also love to burn incense that seeps into my house, causing me a literal headache.

I have tried to bear with them, and have never reported them for anything minor, but the haze from the incense that they were burning gave me breathing difficulties.

I eventually reported them to Town Council (this was before Covid-19 and the coughing incident), they were advised by the officers but nothing concrete was done. To this date, it is a matter of tolerance.

It might irk some to say this, but we are of quite different background. On one hand, they have a lot of gatherings with shady-looking individuals and even set out tables at the shared space to drink liquor in wee hours. Which is not just causing a disturbance but also very worrisome, as being intoxicated usually carries a fair amount of burden (colleteral damage).

To be fair, I have not always been under the opinion that they are troublemakers, but after a sudden visit from the police asking for assistance on locating my neighbour for an investigation and him hiding in his flat until he eventually relented and surrendered, coupled with my observation thus far (with adjustment for confirmation bias) - has led me to the impression that they are not pleasant people to say the least.

Another reason why there is no real action taken by the authorities might be that this could have something to do with religion. Since the burning of the incense, coupled with some very strange visitors going to their unit for what seems to be divine blessings and ringing of the bell all points towards some sort of religious beliefs. Which is a touchy subject in Singapore, a can of worms that no one likes to open. However, I am going to avoid commenting on the religious aspects.

Their actions reveal many deeper underlying issues in Singapore, and perhaps many other countries with social division. To be clear, my family and I are quite poor, and we are definitely in the middle-lower class range. Therefore, I am really glad that I live in Singapore, where if you work hard, you can actually climb out of poverty and build a better life. If you notice, you can broadly stratify people into two groups:

  1. People who are striving for a better life
  2. People who are unwillingly to work/work hard

For the perceptive ones, you may have already noticed this is a simple crabs in a bucket situation, and therefore crab mentality applies. Group 2 people will always try to pull people from Group 1 down - a bold statement, but makes logical sense.

The most dangerous man is the one with nothing to lose.

No matter how rich/influential one may be, you’re only human. Which means you are governed by biological limitations - i.e. illness, death. Covid-19 being a virus, with a relatively high fatality rate as compared to seasonal flu, brings out the worse in Group 2 people. It is, to them an opportunity to exert some control in their downhill life, by spreading the virus, no matter how rich or poor you are, you end up in a russian roulette. And if they lose, they have very little to lose.

This is why the first reaction of Group 1 people is to barricade themselves in their own world. Because of self-destructive behaviour from Group 2 that poses a real threat to society.

I feel that the world has always been this way, but only made even more apparent these days thanks to socmed. I would quote just two recent examples:

  1. Hong Kong Riots (some would say protests but it’s my blog)
  2. Shincheonji - the South Korean church that spreads Covid-19 deliberately

In the first example, it appears to be the HK SAR v The People of HK. But if one would dive deeper, you will find that there is actually a majority of HK citizens against the rioters/protestors. Which brings me to my point, the rioters are Group 2 people, unable to obtain a foothold in society, or a sufficiently comfortable position in life, which leads them to revolt since they have nothing to lose.

In the second example, it shows how easily a person carrying a virus can elect to infect many others and succeed relatively easily. I could also draw emphasis to the brainwashing of the cult-like religion, but I try to keep my blog religion-free.

What are some examples of irresponsible behaviour locally?

Well, for one, in public transport like buses and trains which are the main mode of transport for many Singaporeans. I have witnessed sick people with a bad cough, openly coughing and sneezing without covering their mouth. I have also had a middle-aged man cut his nails and file them right in front of me, but I digress.

The main reason why these people are not practising proper personal hygenie is attributable to the crab mentality.

i.e. Since they are already sick, might as well bring down as many around them as possible.

How can we stop this kind of behaviour? I don’t know, someone please enlighten me.

What is the point of this post?

There isn’t any, this is simply a rant.

However, I might write more about the social division in Singapore and how irresponsible neighours can cause a nuisance leaving their victims to suffer in silence with very little recourse.

Perhaps after the current trial against the duo who alledgedly fatally assaulted an individual who has alledgedly been harrassing them for a prolonged period.

What is interesting are the comments on socmed, where a lot of people empathizes with their predicament and feel that they are the true victims, and the only miscarriage of justice is that the deceased wasn’t stopped by law enforcement, leading to the unfortunate outcome.

I have some thoughts about this that I will write in a separate post.
